Our Best Chefs
Kunal Kapur
Indian celebrity chef
Having served the world with his delicious delicacies, Chef Kunal Kapur is revered by every food connoisseur. An all-in-one Indian celebrity chef, restaurateur, and media personality, Kunal Kapur has helped all of us get a taste of his cooking secrets through his cookbooks.
Clare Smyth
Northern Irish Chef
Clare Smyth catered the “after-party” for the Royal Wedding of Megan and Harry, She was awarded a perfect 10 and the highest new entry in the UK Good Food Guide 2019. Chef Clare is a how she operates her restaurant as running a weekly training day when kitchen staff are given projects to research.
Pankaj Bhadouria
India’s first ever MasterChef.
Life always gives big opportunities in the form of small hints! The big move in Pankaj Bhadouria’s life came when she decided to leave her teaching career, to enter India’s 1st televised cookery reality show – MasterChef India on Star Plus, the leading general entertainment channel.
Pooja Dhingra
Indian pastry chef
Dhingra was born into a family interested in gastronomy.She has published two books. She has been featured in national dailies and is a regular in fashion and lifestyle glossies not just for her abilities in the kitchen, but also as a dynamic businesswoman and inspiration to women
Massimo Bottura
Italian chef
My name is Massimo Bottura. I am an Italian chef born in Modena. I grew up under   the kitchen table at my grandmother   Ancella’s knees. Inspiration comes from the world around me – from art, music, slow food and fast cars. Catch the flash in the dark because it only passes once. Expect the unexpected.
Ranveer Brar
Indian chef
Passion for food has driven me to every nook and corner of the world. From becoming the youngest executive chef at a five star hotel, to hosting my own cooking shows, it is my love for cooking that has got this far. Through this website, I intend to take you on a culinary journey, exploring new places and flavours.
Vishwesh Bhatt
Chef
Vishwesh Bhatt is a James Beard Award-winning chef in Oxford, Bhatt immigrated with his family from Gujarat, India to the United States at the age of 17, first settling in Austin, Texas. He went to college at the University of Kentucky and then to graduate school at the University of Mississippi.
